neural networks learn from examples instead of following a fixed list of rules.
when i explain neural networks to beginners, i usually start from a simple idea.
the model sees an input, makes a prediction, compares that prediction with the answer, and then updates its weights.

an image classifier can learn patterns from handwritten digits.
a convolutional neural network can first look at local regions and then combine them into a larger understanding.
a recurrent neural network can read a sequence step by step and keep part of the earlier context in memory.

these examples are small on purpose.
they are not meant to be state of the art systems.
they are meant to make the training loop visible.
once the training loop is clear, it becomes much easier to understand deeper models and modern architectures.
